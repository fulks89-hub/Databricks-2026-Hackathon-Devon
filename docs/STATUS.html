What Asclepius is

One Databricks App that turns ~10,077 real (but messy, unverified) Indian healthcare facilities into decisions three different people can act on — with every answer backed by cited source text and an honest confidence level. It unifies all four hackathon tracks (Facility Trust Desk, Medical Desert Planner, Referral Copilot, Data Readiness Desk) plus a clinician↔hospital marketplace, a coverage/health Atlas, and a cited AI copilot.

👤 Patient

Find the right care nearby

Enter location + symptoms → matched to facilities with the right discipline and evidence they offer it, on a map with a plain-language confidence level. Never a diagnosis.

🩺 Clinician

Find where you're needed

Free-agent doctors upload a profile → get matched to hospitals whose gaps + surrounding-area health needs fit their discipline.

🏨 Hospital

Fix your coverage gaps

Search available clinicians and upload a staff roster → get recruitment recommendations driven by what's missing + the area's disease/case mix.

Architecture & Databricks stack

One Databricks App (AppKit, React/TypeScript) built on Lakebase Postgres, reading a curated Unity Catalog data layer and powered by Databricks' built-in AI. The mandate (official rules 4.2b) requires a Databricks App on Lakebase + ≥1 additional Databricks tool — we use five capabilities, which drives a strong "Data Relevance" score.

Databricks App (AppKit / React + TS) Lakebase (Postgres 17) Foundation Models Vector Search SQL Warehouse Unity Catalog FDR + NFHS-5 datasets

Flow: Unity Catalog workspace.app_state (curated gold + dims) → synced into Lakebase Postgres app_read (live reads) + native app schema (OLTP persistence) → App serves the three role surfaces; Vector Search + Foundation Models power the cited copilot and claim verification.
